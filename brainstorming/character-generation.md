# Character generation

## Purpose

Generate characters who feel produced by the world rather than pasted into it.

This pipeline should be used for:
- protagonists
- side characters
- factions leaders
- townsfolk
- scholars
- cultists
- mercenaries
- ordinary laborers
- narrators for diegetic artifacts

---

## Core Rule

No character begins as a trope.

Every character begins as:
- a body
- in a place
- at a time
- under institutions
- under resource constraints
- under inherited beliefs
- under social obligations
- with incomplete knowledge

---

## Inputs

Required:
- place
- date
- species
- age band
- social position
- profession or livelihood
- kinship situation
- religious / ideological environment
- major local pressures
- intended narrative role

Optional:
- central contradiction
- desired emotional tone
- desired arc type
- taboo or limit themes to avoid

---

## Output

- character dossier
- material-life summary
- belief map
- obligation map
- capability map
- fear/desire map
- contradiction/tension map
- speech/register notes
- likely story hooks

---

## Phase 0: Material Reality

Define:
- what this character eats
- where they sleep
- what injures them most often
- what they own
- what they cannot legally or materially access
- how far they can travel
- what they owe and to whom
- what bodily capacities or limits their species gives them
- what local climate and terrain do to them

---

## Phase 1: Institutional Embedding

Specify relation to:
- family / clan / household
- law
- religious authority
- employer / guild / lord / state
- military obligation
- debt
- local taboo system
- literacy / schooling
- inheritance

Rule:
A character without institutional embedding is usually just a modern individual in costume.

---

## Phase 2: Epistemic Position

Define:
- what the character knows firsthand
- what they know only by rumor
- what they cannot know
- what they firmly believe but are wrong about
- what words they have for major world phenomena
- what categories they lack entirely

---

## Phase 3: Goal and Pressure Construction

Generate:
- short-term goal
- long-term desire
- unavoidable obligation
- social fear
- private shame
- external pressure
- internal contradiction

Good tensions:
- duty vs appetite
- ritual purity vs survival
- species loyalty vs intimacy across species
- fear of corruption vs relic profit
- law vs kinship
- ambition vs bodily limit
- piety vs evidence
- local identity vs imperial utility

---

## Phase 4: Capability Validation

Check that the character's skills, knowledge, and ambitions are plausible.

Questions:
- how did they learn this?
- what did it cost?
- who taught them?
- what institution enabled or blocked this?
- why is this unusual or ordinary?
- how do body, class, and place shape competence?

Reject characters whose abilities bypass the world model.

---

## Phase 5: Voice and Perception

Define:
- preferred metaphors
- education level
- rhythm of speech
- taboo words
- what they notice first in a room
- what they overlook
- how their species/body affects perception

---

## Final Rule

A good character dossier should make it obvious:
- why this person could only come from this world
- why they are not reducible to exposition delivery
- what parts of the world they naturally reveal
- what parts of the world they will misread
