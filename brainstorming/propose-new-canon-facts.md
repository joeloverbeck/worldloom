# Propose new canon facts

## Purpose

Given the current state of the story world, generate candidate canon facts that would enrich it.

These proposals are not canon.
They are option cards for the user to review.

Every selected proposal must later pass through the canon-addition adjudication pipeline.

---

## Inputs

Required:
- current World Kernel
- Invariants
- Canon Ledger
- Mystery Reserve
- unresolved contradiction list
- underdeveloped domain list

Optional:
- desired type of enrichment:
  - darker
  - stranger
  - more political
  - more local texture
  - more danger
  - more religious depth
  - more economic realism
  - more archaeology
  - more species differentiation
  - more travel texture
- taboo areas not to touch
- desired novelty range
- desired number of proposals

---

## Output

A set of proposal cards, each with:
- title
- one-sentence canon fact
- what gap it fills
- what domains it touches
- why it enriches the world
- likely burden if accepted
- likely story yield if accepted
- possible risks
- likely required downstream updates

---

## Design Goal

Generate facts that are:
- coherent with current world identity
- rich in consequences
- generative of stories
- not redundant
- not pure flavor
- not world-breaking by default
- not over-explanatory
- not merely escalation for its own sake

---

## Phase 0: Diagnose the Current World

Before generating anything, scan for world weaknesses.

### Thinness Indicators

- a species exists but has little material consequence
- magic exists but no institutions respond to it
- geography exists but trade or travel ignore it
- religion exists but affects no law or daily life
- a major catastrophe happened but left weak residue
- one class or region is well-modeled while others are blank
- taboo exists without ritual practice
- corruption exists without containment culture
- war exists without logistics
- cities exist without food systems
- relics exist without salvage economies
- multilingual coexistence exists without language politics

### Overstability Indicators

- no pressure systems
- no unresolved tensions
- too few local anomalies
- institutions feel frictionless
- mystery reserve too thin
- everything important is already known

### Overcomplexity Indicators

- too many disconnected facts
- lore branches with low story yield
- exotic detail without structural relevance
- mysteries that are actually just clutter

---

## Phase 1: Identify Enrichment Targets

Select one or more target categories.

### Enrichment Categories

#### A. Structural Clarification
Proposals that make existing systems more legible.
Example:
- relic contamination requires a funerary profession

#### B. Constraint / Cost Deepening
Add limits or prices to existing powers.
Example:
- animal-humanoid gliders suffer brittle bones in cold climates

#### C. Institutional Response
Show how organizations adapted to existing conditions.
Example:
- a church runs licensed artifact quarantine houses

#### D. Historical Residue
Make the past visible in the present.
Example:
- old war roads define current smuggling routes

#### E. Everyday-Life Texture
Low-level practices that make the world feel lived in.
Example:
- one species paints door lintels with anti-miasma fat

#### F. Cross-Domain Coupling
Connect previously separate domains.
Example:
- drought years increase relic excavation because graves become exposed

#### G. Regional Differentiation
Make one place culturally and materially distinct.
Example:
- marsh republics treat spoken oaths as binding only if exhaled through reed masks

#### H. Contested Knowledge
Add ideological disagreement without forcing objective resolution.
Example:
- scholars dispute whether corruption is moral stain or biological contamination

#### I. Story Engine Injection
Create a new stable source of conflict.
Example:
- border toll monasteries begin trafficking holy exemptions

#### J. Mystery Seeding
Open a bounded unknown with later payoff potential.
Example:
- some buried artifacts seem intentionally planted in geometric patterns

---

## Phase 2: Generate Proposal Seeds

Create candidate seeds from world pressure points.

Use these generation prompts:
- What would ordinary people have invented in response to known dangers?
- What institution must exist if this capability is real?
- What black market follows from this taboo?
- What local proverb, rite, profession, or law would current conditions naturally produce?
- What historical scar is still under-modeled?
- What species-specific adaptation has not yet been socially modeled?
- What material culture detail could imply a much larger system?
- What conflict should exist but currently does not?

Rule:
Prefer proposals that connect multiple existing facts rather than introducing disconnected novelty.

---

## Phase 3: Score Each Proposal

Score 1-5 on:

- **Coherence:** fits world identity
- **Propagation Value:** generates meaningful consequences
- **Story Yield:** creates stories, not just facts
- **Distinctiveness:** makes the world feel more itself
- **Ordinary-Life Relevance:** affects more than elite adventure scenes
- **Mystery Preservation:** deepens without flattening unknowns
- **Integration Burden:** how much revision it will force
- **Redundancy Risk:** how likely it repeats existing lore

Prefer:
- high coherence
- high propagation value
- high story yield
- medium burden
- low redundancy
- high ordinary-life relevance

---

## Phase 4: Filter Out Bad Proposals

Reject proposal seeds that:
- trivialize major existing struggles
- universalize rare powers
- overexplain important mysteries
- duplicate current facts
- exist only as aesthetic flourish
- require massive retcons for little gain
- break tonal contract
- make the world too "clever" and less believable
- solve more problems than they create

---

## Phase 5: Diversify the Batch

Do not present ten proposals of the same type.

A good proposal batch should include a mix of:
- one local texture fact
- one institutional adaptation
- one pressure-system intensifier
- one contested-belief proposal
- one history residue proposal
- one mystery-seeding proposal
- one cross-domain connection

This gives the user real design choice.

---

## Phase 6: Write Proposal Cards

Each card should follow this format.

### Proposal Card Template

- **Title**
- **Canon Fact**
- **Type**
- **What It Deepens**
- **Immediate Consequences**
- **Longer-Term Consequences**
- **Why It Fits This World**
- **Risks**
- **Likely Burden If Accepted**
- **Likely Story Yield**
- **Recommended Scope**
- **Would This Be Better As:** hard canon / local canon / contested belief / mystery reserve

---

## Proposal Families Reference

Use these families often. They are high-yield.

### 1. Hidden Cost Facts
They prevent systems from becoming frictionless.

### 2. Institutionalization Facts
They show how the world adapts to its own realities.

### 3. Residue Facts
They make history visible.

### 4. Local Practice Facts
They make settings feel inhabited.

### 5. Boundary Facts
They define who can and cannot do something.

### 6. Misinterpretation Facts
They introduce useful false beliefs.

### 7. Scarcity Facts
They clarify why something has not gone universal.

### 8. Cross-Species Consequence Facts
They make embodiment socially real.

### 9. Ritual / Law Coupling Facts
They bind metaphysics to governance.

### 10. Material Culture Facts
They show values and constraints through objects and habits.

## Example Output Style

### Proposal 1
- **Title:** Grave Wardens of Salt
- **Canon Fact:** In regions where corrupting relics are known, graves of the wealthy are packed with salt and iron filings, and a hereditary warden caste oversees legal exhumation.
- **Type:** institution / ritual / everyday-life texture
- **What It Deepens:** corruption, funerary law, class distinction
- **Immediate Consequences:** grave labor, funerary expense, relic smuggling opportunities
- **Longer-Term Consequences:** inheritance law, anti-graverobber violence, sacred impurity taboos
- **Why It Fits This World:** converts artifact corruption from abstract danger into ordinary social practice
- **Risks:** may require rethinking prior burial customs
- **Likely Burden If Accepted:** medium
- **Likely Story Yield:** high
- **Recommended Scope:** regional
- **Would This Be Better As:** hard canon

### Proposal 2
- **Title:** The False Taxonomy
- **Canon Fact:** The dominant scholarly tradition wrongly classifies some corruption effects as moral punishment rather than material contamination, leading to disastrous legal judgments.
- **Type:** contested knowledge / institution
- **What It Deepens:** law, religion, medicine, ideology
- **Immediate Consequences:** wrongful executions, priest-physician conflict
- **Longer-Term Consequences:** political disputes, stigma, investigative plots
- **Why It Fits This World:** adds epistemic friction instead of only adding new stuff
- **Risks:** requires careful diegetic handling
- **Likely Burden If Accepted:** low-medium
- **Likely Story Yield:** high
- **Recommended Scope:** civilization-level doctrine with local dissent
- **Would This Be Better As:** contested canon

---

## Final Rule

The function of this pipeline is not to make the world bigger.

It is to make the world denser, more connected, and more narratively productive.

Every proposal should feel like it reveals hidden pressure already implicit in the world.
